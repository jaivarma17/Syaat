Mahaprabhu Ji’s Yoga Camp

Even though yoga has been practised in India for centuries, it has not reached the masses properly. Yoga is not just about moving your hands and legs; it’s a much deeper discipline that requires a guru to guide you to its entirety. That’s exactly what happened during the three days of Mahaprabhu Ji’s yoga camp by Yogacharya Amit Dev at Delhi’s Chhatrasal Stadium, which culminated on Monday evening. During the weekend, dozens of men and women learned yoga techniques that will lead them to a healthier life.

NEW DELHI

At the end of the camp, disciples were full of life. Their joyful faces told the story of transformation and rejuvenation. The camp began with the application of pure cow’s medicated ghee in the nostrils of the disciples—an ancient practice that helps to unblock emotions in the respiratory tract, improve breathing patterns, calm heightened prana in the mind, relax muscles, and induce tranquility.

Several distinguished guests blessed the camp with their presence. Among them was the first daughter of India, Smt. Sharmishtha Mukherjee Ji. Other esteemed guests included AICC’s All India Incharge PC Chacko, BJP Vice President Rajiv Babbar, bodybuilding champion Shashi, Delhi judge Shri Naresh Malhotra Ji, and several Bollywood and television stars.

AIATF Chairman, M.S. Bitta Ji, shared his experience of practicing Mahaprabhu Ji’s yoga under the guidance of Yogacharya Amit Dev Ji for years. He emphasized that our body is a temple of God, and we must preserve it through yoga. He also spoke about various kriyas practised in yoga.

Yogacharya Amit Dev Ji’s family has been dedicated to healing and teaching yoga for generations. His organization, Yog Abhyas Ashram, was established in 1888 by his forefathers, and he continues to carry forward this legacy in the 21st century.

Join the movement and experience the transformative power of yoga with Yogacharya Amit Dev Ji.
